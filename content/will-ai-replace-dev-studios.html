The two reasons a studio exists.

A studio sells capability. It gets chosen for judgement. AI is splitting the two. · July 2026

Everyone's asking whether AI replaces design and dev studios. I asked myself the same. I'd run Ajency, a 50-person product studio, for a decade. Turtlemint acquired us; I served my customary 3 years post-acquisition, and then I was free. Free to test the question for real.

But first: why do studios exist at all?

Why do design and dev studios exist?

To me, Ajency existed for two reasons.

  • Capability: specialists you can't hire or afford full-time in the short term.
  • Judgement + accountability: one owner who holds your whole problem and owns the outcome.

A studio sells capability. But it gets chosen for judgement + accountability. Clients like CIBIL, HealthKart, PayU, Tata Group and OYO chose us for that. So did a lot of early-stage founders. At Ajency, I was the judgement + accountability layer.

Which reason does AI commoditize?

Post-AI, the two reasons move in opposite directions. Capability is commoditizing. Judgement + accountability is amplified.

Can AI agents really replace a team?

So I ran one real project, solo, as a test. Three questions I needed answered:

  • Can AI agents replace a team, for real?
  • Do I have the patience and focus to build alone — QA, UAT, support, the client calls?
  • Can I exist as an independent builder?

The test: a full ERP for a ₹500-crore-plus auto dealer — cash register, DMS and Tally recon, bank and card/UPI recon, dashboards. Discovery to delivery in four months.

In the old model this would be 6–8 team members, one problem split across product, design, backend, frontend, QA. Significant communication overhead.

The solo build with AI agents did replace an entire team. No seagull management, no company to run. Turns out I'd rather build than run a company. Now I get to. 🙂
