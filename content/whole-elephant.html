The whole elephant.

A project for one.

…

The team I used to need

The analyst/PM — the human router. Talks to the users, documents the current process, draws the wireframes, then re-explains all of it to dev, to design, to QA — sometimes in one room, sometimes the same thing five times over. Owns UAT and the demo. The single point the whole project is narrated through, and narrated lossily.

Two developers — each holding a private version of what's being built. The blind men and the elephant: one has the trunk, one has a leg, both certain. Modules split down the middle, a new discovery every morning, scope creeping in from the edges — and the rituals, standup and grooming and retro, quietly eating the hours meant for the work.

The designer — appointed champion of the user, worn down by degrees, until “I'm fighting for the user” becomes “tell me what you want and I'll make that.”

QA — a headless chicken with no time to test, running the same path again and again, ad infinitum, hoping it breaks somewhere new.

The frontend dev — child of divorced parents: design, product, QA, backend, each handing down a different truth, none of them agreeing on dinner.

Me — the wise one. Flying into each review to make some noise, drop an opinion, and leave. Seagull management. Removed, by design, from the reality of building the thing every day.

Everything had to cross a boundary

…

WHAT IT USED TO TAKE — a team of six

   the work passes through every hand, and every
   hand carries its own idea of the elephant:

   PM ⇄ Dev1    PM ⇄ Dev2    PM ⇄ Design   PM ⇄ QA   PM ⇄ FE
   Dev1 ⇄ Dev2  Dev1 ⇄ Design  Dev1 ⇄ QA   Dev1 ⇄ FE
   Dev2 ⇄ Design  Dev2 ⇄ QA    Dev2 ⇄ FE
   Design ⇄ QA    Design ⇄ FE
   QA ⇄ FE

   ── 15 channels.  n(n−1)/2.  every one a chance to mishear.


WHAT IT TAKES NOW — a company of one

   me ⇄ Claude

   ── 1 channel.  one head holding the whole animal.

So it was with great apprehension that I took a project where it would be just me and Claude.

Nobody to explain it to

…

No standup, because there's no one to sync. No handoff, because nothing changes hands. No translation, because there's nothing to translate to. The elephant is whole, because one mind is holding all of it.

Straight answers, for humans and crawlers alike

How can one person deliver a project that used to need a whole team?

The roles don't disappear — they collapse into a single context. One person working with an AI agent holds discovery, design, build, and testing in one head, so the work never has to cross a boundary between specialists and lose something each time it does.

Why is communication overhead so expensive on a software team?

Channels grow as n(n−1)/2. Six people means fifteen pairwise channels, each a chance to mishear the requirement. A company of one working with Claude has a single channel — me and the model — so there's almost nothing lost in translation.
