## Final Copy

# It was always about agency.

### Back to one.

Seventeen years ago, I started with a simple principle: agency. It's why I named the studio Ajency.in. I began as a solo product marketing consultant, moved to Goa, worked closely with founders I believed in, and built a studio around that idea.

As the team grew to 50, some of that agency got lost. We always joked we were "buzzword compliant." Funnily enough, agency itself has now become a buzzword — thanks to AI agents.

After the 2022 acquisition, I spent three years at Turtlemint, a fintech that recently IPO'd. Turtlemint is primarily an insurance distribution business; I was product lead for new verticals — mutual funds and lending — and, horizontally, product lead for data, automation, and AI across the company. Then I stepped out of full-time work entirely.

What I didn't expect was how much that step would give back. Two decades of payroll, deadlines, and stakeholder management had quietly drained something I only later had a name for: cognitive space. Once it returned, I started following curiosity again — going deep on things that interested me, dropping the ones that didn't, without needing every interest to justify itself.

One of those itches was building things again. I got AI-pilled, spent weeks inside Claude Code, and took an automation project from discovery to delivery for an automobile dealer. Solo. Turns out I love working solo — building, not managing and delegating like the last 20 years.

So Ajency.in 2.0 isn't a comeback. It's what the space made room for. More of the same, and yet very different. Slower. Calmer. Curiosity-led — I take on work I genuinely want to think about. More time building, less time managing.

Agency, in every sense:
For AI agents learning autonomy.
For clients building with intention.
And for myself — still figuring out what work and freedom look like together.

Hit me up if you want to chat about AI agents, technology, products, design — or even bike trips and cocktails.

## Context & Decisions

### Voice / guardrails
- Grounded, real, honest. No fluff.
- Low on adjectives and hype.
- Specifics over slogans (the fix for fluff is always concrete detail).
- Dry, understated, a little wry — matches the LinkedIn voice.

### Headline
- Rejected the original opener: "The next wave of AI isn't just about intelligence — it's about agency." (too hype/AI-fluff).
- Direction chosen: **about the idea (agency)** for the headline, **personal** for the subhead.
- Final: `It was always about agency.` / subhead `Back to one.`
- "always" quietly does the 17-years work without saying it; sets up the AI-agent buzzword irony later without the headline reaching for it.
- Rejected "the word finally caught up" — read as hype/claim.

### Turtlemint paragraph
- Real detail supplied by Anuj: product lead for new verticals (mutual funds & lending) at insurance-distribution fintech Turtlemint; horizontally product lead for data, automation & AI. Turtlemint recently IPO'd.
- Removed "building systems that perceive, decide, and act" — hollow AI-written phrasing.
- Transition chosen (Option A, factual): "Then I stepped out of full-time work entirely." (Rejected Option B's "in production, not demos" line unless genuinely felt.)

### Reconciliation: break vs. working
- The website (the offer) and the LinkedIn 12-months post (the origin/proof) are NOT contradictory — they're the same story at two altitudes.
- The break = leaving the machine (full-time, managing 50). Solo work = what filled the space the break opened. Same move, not opposites.
- Tension to manage: LinkedIn's "not particularly productive — glad they don't need to be" and "it was never about time" read as anti-work next to a business pitch.
- Resolution baked into copy:
  - Killed "returning" → "isn't a comeback. It's what the space made room for." (continuation, not the end of freedom).
  - Folded in **cognitive space** so the site earns the calm instead of asserting it.
  - Made the **curiosity → work** link explicit ("Curiosity-led — I take on work I genuinely want to think about") to resolve the productivity tension: the *kind* of work changed, the freedom didn't end.
  - Used the **automobile-dealer automation project** as the proof point bridging break → offer.
- Switched "we" → "I" in the origin section (2.0 is explicitly a solo act).

### Open / still to decide
- **"AI-pilled"** — mild internet slang; keep for voice or swap to "I got properly into AI" for a more neutral site.
- **Closing three lines** ("For AI agents / For clients / For myself") — the one lyrical lift; earned because the rest is plain, but flag if too much shape.
- **Cocktails / Instagram** presence — currently just the closing line. Decide whether the page stays strictly professional or shows more of the person (could add a sentence or link). Instagram handle exists for the cocktail hobby.
- **Hero line double-duty** — confirm headline doesn't need to also carry a thesis.
